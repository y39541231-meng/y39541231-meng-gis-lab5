Processing choices and challenges

Key decisions

The following choices were made to keep the workflow reproducible, reduce processing load, and make the outputs suitable for website presentation.

Large raster size

Layers were clipped to the Italy case-study boundary and saved as GeoTIFF or GeoPackage outputs.

Resolution mismatch

CAMS pollutant rasters and ESRI land-cover rasters had different spatial resolutions, so resampling was required before zonal statistics.

NoData handling

NoData values were preserved outside the area of interest and controlled during reclassification and masking.

Cloud transitions

Land-cover transitions involving class 10, Clouds, were excluded from stable, gain, and loss statistics.

Heavy vector layers

Polygonized masks were dissolved by transition zone or pollutant class to reduce feature count before computing statistics.

Chart production

Pie charts were inserted as exported figures, allowing the website to show exposure results even when plotting plugins were unavailable.

Main processing challenge

The most demanding part of the workflow was the high-resolution land-cover processing, especially after converting raster masks into vector features.

01 Large land-cover files

The ESRI 10 m land-cover datasets produced very large intermediate raster files, often reaching tens of GB.

02 Millions of vector features

After polygonizing the land-cover change mask, the attribute table contained around 6–7 million features.

03 Slow dissolve operation

The dissolve step became the main bottleneck, and some group processing tasks required more than ten hours to complete.
